Impressionism in Music and Art: Interdisciplinary Connections

4th Grade-8th Grade

During this unit students:

  • Compare and contrast Impressionist music and art via aural and visual examples
  • Create an Impressionist painting inspired by Claire de la Lune
  • Learn about impressionist composer's techniques via listening, visual piano examples, playing and composition experiences

Mozart/Twinkle Twinkle Little Star Unit

Kindergarten-5th Grade

During this unit students:

  • Introduced as a mystery song (students guess the song based on its melody)
  • Learn about Mozart, what a composer does
  • Learn about rhythm: echo clap, antiphon, track steady beat and rhythm, write rhythm on board
  • 2nd Grade: Song dotted Twinkle Twinkle melody (student work example below)
  • Experience theme and variation form by listening and labeling sections
  • 5th grade: Compose short theme and variation piece (write 4 measure theme, create 4 measure variation by changing rhythm or adding notes)

Halloween Tone Poem Unit

During this unit students:

  • Learn about the tone poem Danse Macabre and Camille Saint-Saens

Peter and the Wolf Unit

Kindergarten-4th Grade

During this unit students:

  • Learn about musical themes through the Peter and the Wolf visual story
  • Match musical themes to instruments
  • Play rhythmic themes on classroom percussion instruments to a SpongeBob story
  • 3rd and 4th grade: Write their own short story and composed short musical themes for each character in the story

White Stripes Unit

Kindergarten-4th Grade

During this unit students:

  • Discussed types of musical ensembles
  • Read a book based on the "We are Going to be Friends" song
  • Learn about the White Stripes band and their instruments (guitar, drum set)
  • Compare/contrast performances of "We are are Going to be Friends"
  • Performed "We are Going to be Friends" on xylophone and hand drums. (Demonstrated in the video below)

Jazz Unit

Kindergarten-4th Grade

During this unit students:

  • Relate music to literature through reading and responding to "The Jazz Fly" and "Pete the Cat and the New Guy"
  • Discover what makes jazz music sound unique through listening and responding to music
  • Learn about and play the drum set

5th- 8th Grade Band

During this class students:

  • Began 5th Grade students on flute, clarinet, saxophone, trumpet, trombone and percussion using sound before symbol approach
  • Emphasized fundamental skills and critical thinking skills in rehearsal
  • Performed a Spring Band Concert

Middle School General Music

During this class students:

  • Performed Bucket Drumming
  • Music Advocacy: Create a poster about why music is important using facts from music research
  • Music Appreciation: Listening and discussing Jazz, Rock, Hip Hop and Electronic music - link to PowerPoint here
  • Build their own musical instruments
